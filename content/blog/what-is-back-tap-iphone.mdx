Your iPhone has a button you've probably never used: the entire back of the phone. Back Tap is an accessibility feature that detects a double or triple tap on the back of the device — even through most cases — and triggers any action you assign: a screenshot, the flashlight, muting, or any Shortcut. It's been built into every iPhone since the iPhone 8 (on iOS 14 and later), and most people have never turned it on.

## How to enable Back Tap

1. Open **Settings**.
2. Go to **Accessibility → Touch**.
3. Scroll to the bottom and tap **Back Tap**.
4. Choose **Double Tap** or **Triple Tap**, then pick the action.

That's the whole setup. The tap detection uses the accelerometer, so it works through cases (very thick or wallet-style cases can dampen it) and doesn't require any specific spot — anywhere on the back registers.

## What people assign to it

The stock options cover system actions: screenshot, flashlight, volume, mute, Control Center, magnifier, accessibility toggles. But the feature gets genuinely powerful at the bottom of the list, where your **Shortcuts** appear. Any automation you can build, the back of your phone can trigger: opening the camera to a specific mode, starting a timer, sending a preset message — or logging an expense.

## The killer use case: two-second expense logging

Expense tracking dies from friction: unlock, find the app, open it, tap through a form. Back Tap removes almost all of it. Assign the double tap to your expense tracker — or to a Shortcut that opens it directly in recording mode — and the flow becomes: double-tap the back of the phone, say or confirm the expense, done. About two seconds, no icon hunting, works the moment you step away from the register.

## Troubleshooting

Back Tap not responding? Check the case first — thick or rigid cases absorb taps; a firmer, quicker double-tap usually registers. Accidental triggers when setting the phone down? Switch from double to triple tap, which almost never fires by accident. And if the option is missing entirely, you're either on an iPhone older than the 8 or an iOS before 14.

## FAQ

### Which iPhones support Back Tap?

iPhone 8 and newer, running iOS 14 or later. It's a software feature using the accelerometer, not a special sensor — which is why it works on the whole back surface and through most cases.

### Does Back Tap drain the battery?

No measurable impact. The motion detection it uses runs as part of the system's existing sensor pipeline; enabling Back Tap doesn't add a background process you'd notice.

### Can Back Tap run any app?

Directly, it triggers system actions and Shortcuts. To open a specific app, create a one-action Shortcut ("Open App") and assign that. Apps designed for it — like Lumi's recording mode — can land you on the exact screen you need instead of just the app's front door.
