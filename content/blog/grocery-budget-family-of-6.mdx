**A family of six's groceries run roughly $1,550/month on the USDA Thrifty tier to about $2,550 on Liberal in 2026 — at six people, food rivals housing as the budget's top line, and a 15% improvement is worth $250–380 every month.** Here's the benchmark table and the math that matters at this scale.

## The benchmarks for a family of 6

| Plan | Monthly cost (approx.) | Weekly |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| Thrifty | ~$1,550 | ~$360 |
| Low-Cost | ~$1,700 | ~$390 |
| Moderate | ~$2,100 | ~$485 |
| Liberal | ~$2,550 | ~$590 |

Derived from [USDA Food Plans](https://www.fna.usda.gov/research/cnpp/usda-food-plans/cost-food-monthly-reports), which price per-person by age (the USDA published $1,507–2,462 for a reference six-person family in early 2024; 2026 figures run modestly higher with food inflation). Ages dominate: six with four little kids eats hundreds less than six with three teenagers — check your own composition against the per-person tables before judging your number.

## At six, groceries are infrastructure

The scale changes the toolkit. Warehouse clubs and discount grocers (Aldi-first shopping alone [commonly saves 20%+](https://www.ramseysolutions.com/budgeting/average-cost-of-groceries)) become the default, not the trick; a chest freezer pays for itself in months (sale-priced meat and bread at scale); once-a-month bulk trips plus weekly produce top-ups beat the five-store weekly scramble; and cooking doubles — six servings tonight, six frozen — turns one hour into two dinners. Waste is the villain worth naming: 15% of a $2,000 spend is $300/month, so the freezer-inventory-before-shopping rule outearns every coupon in the drawer. ## FAQ

**Is $1,800 a month normal for a family of 6?** Yes — solidly between Low-Cost and Moderate. With multiple teens it reads as Low-Cost discipline.

**What's a realistic weekly budget for 6?** $360–490 for most six-person households; under $360 is committed Thrifty-tier planning, $590+ is Liberal-tier defaults.

**Does a bigger family mean cheaper per person?** Yes — USDA applies economies-of-scale adjustments, and bulk mechanics amplify them: per-person costs at six run 10–20% below a [family of four's](/blog/grocery-budget-family-of-4).
