**Fleur and Lumi share the same foundation — privacy-first iPhone trackers with no bank login and no forced account — and diverge on what they optimize: Fleur makes manual logging pleasant with a genuinely beautiful interface, Lumi makes logging disappear with voice input, Apple Pay auto-import, and receipt scanning.** Both are honest products; the choice is about which keeps *you* logging in month three.

## Quick comparison

| | Lumi | Fleur |
|---|------|-------|
| Design philosophy | Function-first: capture in seconds | Aesthetic-first: budgeting that's pleasant to look at |
| Entry methods | Voice, Apple Pay auto-import, receipt scan, manual | Manual (quick, well-designed forms) |
| AI | Speech parsing, receipt vision, month-end forecast | None |
| Bank login | Never | Never |
| Account required | No | No — iCloud sync built in |
| Budgets | Category limits, live progress, pre-overspend warnings | Monthly budget + insights |
| Multi-currency | 150+, live conversion | Basic |
| Pricing model | Free / $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr subscription | Free / one-time premium purchase, no subscription |
| App Store rating | — | 4.8 (11K+ reviews) |

## Where Fleur wins

Fleur understood something the finance industry didn't: people avoid budgeting apps because they look like tax software. Its cute, minimal design turns logging into a small pleasant ritual, the 4.8-star rating across 11K+ reviews is earned, and the pricing model is arguably the fairest in the category — a one-time premium purchase instead of a subscription. For people who *enjoy* the manual ritual and want to pay once, Fleur is an easy recommendation.

## Where Lumi wins

The ritual is the risk. Even a beautiful form is a form, and busy weeks are when manual systems quietly die — [most tracking habits collapse within weeks](/blog/how-to-track-spending-that-actually-sticks). Lumi removes the moment of friction entirely: say ["groceries forty dollars"](/blog/how-to-log-expenses-without-typing), let [Apple Pay purchases log themselves](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending), point the camera at a receipt. Add serious multi-currency (150+ with live conversion) and an [AI month-end forecast](/blog/how-to-stop-overspending), and Lumi is the pick when you want the data complete even on the weeks you forget the app exists.

## Which one should you pick?

You find logging meditative, want maximum cuteness, and prefer paying once: Fleur. You want tracking that survives your least-disciplined weeks, travel across currencies, or a forecast of where the month is heading: Lumi. Either way you're keeping bank credentials out of it — both apps are on the right side of [the no-bank-login divide](/blog/best-expense-trackers-no-bank-login-2026).

## FAQ

### Is Fleur really free?

The core app is free with a one-time premium upgrade for extra features — no subscription, which reviewers consistently praise. Lumi's free tier includes its AI features (voice input, 10 receipt scans/month, Apple Pay import); Premium is $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr.

### Does either app connect to my bank?

No — both are manual-first and privacy-first by design. The difference is that Lumi layers automation (voice, Shortcut import, scanning) on top of the no-bank model.

### Which app is better for couples or travel?

Travel: Lumi, for 150+ currencies with live conversion ([how to track multiple currencies](/blog/how-to-track-multiple-currencies)). Couples: neither has shared wallets; the separate-tracking-plus-settle-up model works with both — see [best budget apps for couples](/blog/best-budget-apps-for-couples-2026).

### Can an aesthetic app really change budgeting behavior?

Design that reduces dread genuinely helps people start — Fleur's reviews prove it. What design can't fix is entry cost on busy days; that's an automation problem, which is the gap Lumi targets.
