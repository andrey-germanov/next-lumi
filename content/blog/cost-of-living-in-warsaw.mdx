**A single person in Warsaw needs about 6,500–8,000 PLN net per month (~€1,510–1,860) for realistic living in 2026, and 9,000–11,000 PLN for genuine comfort — a one-bedroom near the center, regular dining out, gym, and savings.** Warsaw has become Central Europe's priciest capital, but the quality-per-złoty remains strong. Here's the breakdown.

## Rent: the line that grew up

Warsaw rents crossed the psychological threshold years ago: ~4,900 PLN for a central one-bedroom, ~3,900 outside. The value plays are Praga (across the river, gentrifying fast), Mokotów's non-metro edges, and Wola one stop past the towers. The 180 PLN ZTM pass makes "outside the center" a 15-minute compromise, not a lifestyle downgrade — Warsaw's transit is the budget's best friend.

## Food: Biedronka economics

The 800 PLN grocery baseline assumes Biedronka and Lidl doing the heavy lifting; the same basket at Żabka convenience prices runs half again more. Lunch deals (30–45 PLN) keep eating out sane, but Warsaw's booming restaurant scene — and Pyszne delivery — is where budgets drift 1,000 PLN without anyone deciding anything. That's [discretionary spending](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) at work; a [category limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit) on delivery makes the drift visible before it compounds.

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Warsaw?

6,500–8,000 PLN net/month for realistic single living in 2026; 9,000–11,000 PLN for comfort with savings. Bare survival is possible around 5,500 PLN.

### Is Warsaw expensive in 2026?

By Central European standards, yes — it's the region's priciest capital, with rents that surprise arrivals from Kraków or Wrocław. Against Berlin or Amsterdam, it still undercuts significantly at similar quality.

### How much is rent in Warsaw?

~4,900 PLN ($1,224) for a central one-bedroom, ~3,900 PLN ($967) outside the center; a 40–59 m² two-room flat averages 3,895 PLN citywide per Otodom.

### What salary is comfortable in Warsaw?

Around 9,000 PLN net (~13,000 gross on employment contract) buys comfort for one person; 14,000 net is "living well" territory. Keep rent under 40% of net and the rest follows the [50/30/20 logic](/tools/50-30-20-budget-calculator).
