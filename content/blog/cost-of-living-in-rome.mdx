**A comfortable single life in Rome runs €2,100–2,500 per month in 2026 — about €852 of day-to-day costs plus rent that swings from €800 in Monteverde to €1,800+ in Trastevere.** Rome rewards those who live like Romans and taxes those who live like tourists. Here's the honest breakdown.

## Rome monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost |
|----------|--------------|

## Rent: the quartiere decision

Trastevere's cobblestones cost €1,200–1,800+ for a one-bedroom; Monteverde — one tram ride up the hill — does €800–1,100 for more space and actual grocery stores. San Giovanni, Pigneto, and EUR follow the same logic: Rome's charm is city-wide, but its rent premium is concentrated in five photogenic neighborhoods. The €35 ATAC pass makes the connected quartieri a lifestyle upgrade disguised as a compromise.

## Food: the mercato vs aperitivo ledger

Groceries at €200–300 assume the neighborhood mercato and Conad doing the base work — Italian food quality at these prices is the country's quiet subsidy. The other side of the ledger: €10 aperitivi, €25 trattoria dinners, and tourist-zone espresso at triple price. ## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Rome?

€2,100–2,500/month for a comfortable single life including rent in 2026. Choosing Monteverde over Trastevere alone moves the total by €400–700.

### Is Rome expensive in 2026?

Mid-pack for Western Europe: cheaper than Milan, Paris, or Amsterdam, pricier than Iberian or Central European capitals. The tourist economy inflates everything within sight of a monument — locals' Rome costs meaningfully less.

### How much is rent in Rome?

€1,200–1,800+ in prime picturesque areas; €800–1,100 in well-connected quartieri like Monteverde and EUR; ~€790 average outside the center.

### What salary do you need for Rome?

Around €1,850+ net covers a modest comfortable life; €2,500+ allows the full version with savings. Italian utilities spike November–March — budget heating seasonally.
