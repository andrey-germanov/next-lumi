Search "best free budgeting app" and half the results are apps with a 7-to-34-day trial that quietly need a credit card and a monthly charge afterward. That's not free, that's a funnel. This list only includes apps with a genuine free tier you can use indefinitely — plus, at the end, a quick note on which "free" apps are actually free trials in disguise, so you don't get surprised.

## What counts as "actually free" here

We only counted an app as free if it has a tier that works forever, with no credit card required and no forced expiration. Apps that offer a 7-30 day trial and then require payment are noted separately below, because they show up in "free" searches constantly and it's worth knowing upfront.

## Quick comparison — genuinely free tiers

| App | What's free forever | Bank login required | Main limitation on free tier |
|-----|----------------------|----------------------|-------------------------------|
| Lumi | 10 AI scans/mo, 2 budgets, Back Tap, Apple Pay import, voice input, basic analytics | No | Limited AI scans and budgets |
| Goodbudget | Envelope budgeting, up to 20 envelopes | No (manual entry) | 20-envelope cap, 1 device |
| PocketGuard | "In My Pocket" spending number, basic tracking | Yes | Limited categorization depth |
| EveryDollar | Manual zero-based budgeting | No (manual only) | No bank sync without upgrading |
| Chime | Banking + automatic round-up savings | N/A — it's the bank | Not a dedicated budgeting app |

## Lumi — Free tier built around fast logging, not restrictions

Lumi's free plan isn't a stripped-down demo — it includes the features that actually save time day to day: Back Tap logging, Apple Pay auto-import, voice input, 10 AI receipt scans a month, 2 category budgets, and basic analytics, all with no bank login and no credit card at signup. The ceiling is AI scan volume and budget count, not core functionality.

**Best for:** Anyone who wants fast, low-friction logging without connecting a bank account or starting a trial clock.

## Goodbudget — Free envelope budgeting, no bank required

Goodbudget's free tier gives you the full envelope-budgeting method with up to 20 envelopes on one device, entirely manual entry, no bank connection ever required. It's one of the only apps here that's both free and privacy-first by design, not just as a free-tier limitation.

**Best for:** People who want the classic envelope method for free and are fine entering transactions by hand.

## PocketGuard — Free "safe to spend" tracking

PocketGuard's free tier gives you the core "In My Pocket" number and basic transaction tracking, though it does require linking a bank account to calculate it. Categorization and deeper insights are more limited than the paid tier, but the core feature works without paying.

**Best for:** People who want one simple spendable number and are comfortable linking their bank for it.

## EveryDollar — Free zero-based budgeting, manual entry

EveryDollar's free tier is fully functional zero-based budgeting with manual transaction entry — no bank sync, which is a paid-only feature, but you're not blocked from using the core budgeting method for free indefinitely.

**Best for:** People following zero-based budgeting who are fine typing in transactions themselves.

## Chime — Free banking with automatic savings

Chime isn't a budgeting app in the traditional sense — it's a full banking app (checking + savings) with automatic round-up transfers to savings built in, at no monthly cost. If you're open to using it as your primary bank account, the automated savings piece is genuinely free, which most automated-savings competitors are not.

**Best for:** People who want free automated round-up savings and don't mind switching their everyday bank account to get it.

## The ones that look free but aren't

**YNAB** offers a 34-day free trial, then requires a paid subscription (~$109/year) — there is no free-forever tier. **Monarch Money** offers a 7-day trial, then a paid subscription (~$100/year). **Copilot** similarly trial-only, ~$96/year after. **Digit** and **Acorns** (covered in our [financial goals apps comparison](/blog/best-apps-for-financial-goals-2026)) are subscription-only with no meaningful free tier. None of these are bad apps — they're covered in our [full budget app comparison](/blog/best-budget-apps-2026) — but if "free" is the deciding factor for you, they don't qualify.

## Bottom line

If you want fast logging without linking a bank, start with **Lumi**'s free tier. If you want the classic envelope method for free, **Goodbudget**. If you want one clear "safe to spend" number and don't mind bank sync, **PocketGuard**. If you're following zero-based budgeting and want to enter transactions by hand, **EveryDollar**. And if you want your bank account itself to save money for you automatically at no cost, switch to **Chime**.
