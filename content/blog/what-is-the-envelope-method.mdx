**The envelope method is a budgeting system where you divide cash into labeled envelopes by spending category, and once an envelope is empty you stop spending in that category until the next month.** Recently revived on social media as "cash stuffing," it works by making your spending limits physical: you can see the money shrink, and there is no swiping past a balance you cannot see.

## How it works

At the start of the month you withdraw cash for your variable spending and split it into envelopes, one per category. When an envelope runs dry, that category is done for the month.

Say you budget the following in cash each month:

- **Groceries — 400 dollars**
- **Dining out — 150 dollars**
- **Gas — 120 dollars**
- **Fun and entertainment — 100 dollars**

You put exactly that cash in each labeled envelope. Buy 45 dollars of groceries and the envelope drops to 355 dollars. When the dining envelope reaches zero on the 22nd, you cook at home for the rest of the month rather than pulling from another envelope. If you have cash left in an envelope at month-end, you can roll it into savings or a [sinking fund](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund).

Fixed bills like rent and utilities stay out of the envelopes; those are paid directly. Envelopes are for the flexible, easy-to-overspend categories.

## Why it matters

The envelope method works because it turns an abstract limit into something you can feel. A number in an app is easy to ignore; an empty envelope is not. Studies of spending behavior have long suggested people spend more freely with cards than with cash, and the envelope system leans directly into that psychology. It is especially effective if your problem is impulse spending rather than math.

The most common mistake is "borrowing" from one envelope to cover another. The moment you do that, the hard stop disappears and the system stops protecting you. A second drawback is practical: carrying and managing physical cash is inconvenient, risky if lost, and impossible for online purchases. That is why many people now use the digital version, keeping "envelopes" as category budgets in an app so they get the same hard limits without the cash. If you also want to save aggressively, pair the method with a [savings goal calculator](/tools/savings-goal-calculator) to decide what leftover envelope cash should fund.
