**A single expat in Mexico City spends about $1,600–2,200 per month in 2026 for a comfortable life, and $900–1,400 is workable in outer neighborhoods with local habits.** The spread between those numbers is mostly one decision: which colonia you rent in. Here's the honest breakdown in pesos and dollars.

## Mexico City monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (MXN) | ~USD |
|----------|--------------------|------|

## The Roma-Condesa premium

Mexico City has a two-tier rental market: the international bubble (Roma Norte, Condesa, Polanco) at 20,000–35,000 MXN for a one-bedroom, and everywhere else at half that. Narvarte, Escandón, or Del Valle sit fifteen minutes away with the same cafés minus the Instagram crowd — the single biggest lever in a CDMX budget. Rents are climbing ~9% a year, so whatever you sign, budget the increase in advance as a [sinking fund](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund).

## Food: markets vs the delivery trap

Local markets keep groceries at $250–300/month; leaning on imported brands pushes $300–400. The real leak is delivery — Rappi's convenience quietly doubles a food budget one 180-peso order at a time, the textbook [impulse spending](/blog/what-is-impulse-spending) pattern. Cash still rules at markets and taquerías, which is exactly the spending that vanishes from memory — [tracking cash](/blog/how-to-track-cash-spending) is where CDMX budgets get honest, and a two-second voice log ("tacos ochenta pesos") is all it takes.

## FAQ

### How much do you need to live in Mexico City?

$1,600–2,200/month for a comfortable single-person life in 2026; $900–1,400 in outer neighborhoods with local habits. The rent decision drives most of the difference.

### Is Mexico City still affordable in 2026?

Outside the international bubble, yes — food, transport, and services remain far below US prices. Inside Roma-Condesa, rents now rival mid-tier US cities and climb ~9% yearly.

### How much is rent in Mexico City?

10,000–18,000 MXN ($555–1,000) for a one-bedroom in most decent areas; 20,000–35,000 MXN ($1,110–1,940) in Roma, Condesa, and Polanco.

### Is Mexico City safe to budget cash for?

Cash remains essential for markets and street food. Keep a weekly cash [spending limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit), withdraw once a week, and log as you spend — untracked cash is the biggest gap in most CDMX budgets.
