**Expense tracking** is the habit of recording every purchase you make so you can see exactly where your money goes. Each expense is logged with an amount, a date, and a category, then reviewed over time to reveal spending patterns. It is the raw data that makes budgeting, saving, and smarter money decisions possible.

## How it works

Every time you spend, you capture it: 4.50 for coffee, 62 for groceries, 15 for a streaming service. Each entry gets a category such as food, transport, or entertainment. At the end of the week or month, you total each category and compare the results against your plan or your expectations.

The insight usually comes from the totals, not the individual purchases. You might feel like dining out is a minor line item, then discover you spent 340 dollars on it last month. That is the power of tracking: it turns a vague sense of your spending into precise numbers you can act on. Once you see the pattern, you can decide what to cut, what to keep, and where to redirect the savings.

## Why it matters

You cannot manage what you do not measure. Studies consistently find that people who track their spending save more and feel more in control of their finances. Tracking also catches the quiet money leaks, forgotten subscriptions, creeping delivery fees, and impulse buys that never register until they show up in a monthly total.

The biggest mistake is trying to track everything by hand and quitting after a few days. Manual logging is tedious, and one missed day snowballs into a whole abandoned habit. The fix is to make capture so fast and low-friction that it happens automatically, in the moment, before the detail is forgotten.
