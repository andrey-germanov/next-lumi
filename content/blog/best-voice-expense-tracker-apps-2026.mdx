Typing is why expense tracking dies. Every purchase becomes a form: amount, merchant, category, date. Voice removes the form — you say "coffee, five euros" and move on with your life. But "voice expense tracker" can mean three very different things in 2026, and they are not equally good. We tested all three approaches so you don't have to.

## The three ways to track expenses by voice

**Native AI voice input** — the app itself listens and parses natural speech into a structured expense: amount, merchant, category. No setup, works like talking to a person.

**Siri Shortcuts** — you build an automation that asks for input when triggered by "Hey Siri" or a tap. Works with almost any app that accepts Shortcuts input, but you assemble it yourself and the parsing is rigid.

**Plain dictation** — tapping the microphone on the iOS keyboard inside any expense app's note field. Technically voice, but you still navigate the form; it only replaces the typing itself.

The difference in practice: native AI input takes one sentence; a Siri Shortcut takes a trigger phrase plus a couple of prompts; dictation saves you almost nothing.

## What to look for in a voice expense tracker

Natural-language parsing, not command syntax — "coffee 5 euros" should just work, without memorizing a phrase order. Multi-language support if you don't live your whole life in English. On-the-spot categorization, so you're not sorting a pile of uncategorized entries on Sunday. And no bank login as the price of convenience: voice input exists precisely so an app doesn't need to read your bank account to know what you spent.

## FAQ

### Does Siri have a built-in expense tracker?

No. Siri can run Shortcuts that write to an expense app or a spreadsheet, but there's no native expense tracking in iOS. The closest built-in view is Wallet's transaction history for Apple Pay purchases, which doesn't cover cash or cards used physically.

### Is voice input accurate for accents and other languages?

Modern AI parsing is far more forgiving than the old dictation engines. Lumi understands natural phrases in multiple languages — the same sentence structure works in English, German, Spanish, Ukrainian, or Russian — and you can always glance at the parsed entry before confirming.

### Is voice faster than scanning a receipt?

They solve different moments. Voice wins when there's no paper — cash, transit, splitting dinner. Scanning wins when the receipt has ten line items you'd never dictate. A good setup uses voice for quick entries and [receipt scanning](/blog/best-receipt-scanner-apps-2026) for itemized ones.
