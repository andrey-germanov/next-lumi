**The standard benchmark says: by 30, have 1x your annual salary saved across all accounts — retirement, investments, and cash combined.** Earn $60,000? Target $60,000 saved. The reality check: the median American household under 35 has $5,400 in savings and about $18,880 in retirement accounts, so the typical 30-year-old is well short. Here's what the benchmark really means and how the catch-up math works.

## The 1x benchmark, unpacked

[Fidelity's guideline](https://www.fidelity.com/viewpoints/retirement/retirement-guidelines) — 1x salary by 30, on the way to 10x by 67 — counts *everything*: 401(k) including employer match, IRA, brokerage, and savings accounts. It assumes you started saving ~15% of income at 25. Missed that start? The multiple isn't a verdict, it's a slope — what matters at 30 is your savings *rate*, because you still have 35+ compounding years (see the math in the [compound interest calculator](/tools/compound-interest-calculator)).

## Behind at 30? The catch-up math

Suppose you earn $60k and have $15k saved — a $45k gap. Closing it by 35 (to hit [2x by 35](/blog/how-much-savings-by-35)... a $105k total gap) needs roughly $1,400/month saved; just reaching 1x by 33 needs about $1,200/month. Steep — which is why the fix is rarely "try harder" and usually "find the leak." Three moves, in order:

1. **Find your real number.** Track one complete month. Most people discover $150–400/month in [subscriptions they forgot](/blog/how-to-track-subscriptions), [impulse buys](/blog/what-is-impulse-spending), and [lifestyle creep](/blog/what-is-lifestyle-creep) that crept in with each raise. Make tracking frictionless or it won't survive the month — [voice logging](/blog/how-to-log-expenses-without-typing) and [automatic Apple Pay import](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending) in [Lumi](/) exist for exactly this.
2. **Automate the redirect.** Whatever the month reveals becomes an automatic payday transfer — [pay yourself first](/blog/what-is-pay-yourself-first), before spending happens.
3. **Escalate with income.** Each raise: half to savings rate, half to life. This single rule is how 1x at 33 becomes [3x by 40](/blog/how-much-savings-by-40) without pain.

## FAQ

**Does my employer 401(k) match count toward 1x?** Yes — the multiple includes all retirement assets, employer contributions included.

**Is $50,000 saved at 30 good?** It's roughly 10x the median for your age group. Against the benchmark it depends on salary: at $50k income you're exactly on target; at $100k you're halfway.

**Should the 1x be cash or invested?** Mostly invested. Keep 3–6 months of expenses liquid as an [emergency fund](/blog/what-is-an-emergency-fund); parking the rest in cash forfeits the compounding that makes the later multiples reachable.
