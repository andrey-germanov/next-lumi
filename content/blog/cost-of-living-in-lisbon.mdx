**Living in Lisbon in 2026 costs a single person roughly €1,750–2,150 per month including rent — about €745–865 of day-to-day expenses plus €1,000–1,400 for a one-bedroom apartment.** Daily life stays affordable by Western European standards; rent is the line that decides everything. Here's the honest breakdown.

## Lisbon monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (one person) |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Rent, 1-bedroom city center | ~€1,200–1,500 |
| Rent, 1-bedroom outside center | ~€1,000 |
| Studio near center | ~€600–900 |
| Groceries | ~€280 |
| Utilities (apartment) | ~€152 |
| Public transport pass | €40 |
| Everything else (per Numbeo, ex-rent total) | ~€745–865 |
| **Realistic total** | **~€1,750 (outside center) – €2,150 (center)** |

Sources: [Numbeo Lisbon](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Lisbon), [Expatistan](https://www.expatistan.com/cost-of-living/lisbon) — figures as of mid-2026.

## Rent: the budget-maker

Everything else in Lisbon is negotiable; rent isn't. A center one-bedroom at €1,200–1,500 consumes most of a €2,000 budget by itself, while the same money outside the center (Benfica, Alvalade, across the river in Almada) leaves €400+ of monthly slack. The practical rule for arrivals: keep rent under 40% of your Lisbon budget or the rest of this article won't help. For the framework, see the [50/30/20 rule](/blog/what-is-the-50-30-20-rule) — and accept that in Lisbon, "needs" will lean heavy.

## Food, transport, and daily life

Groceries for one run ~€280/month from Continente or Pingo Doce, noticeably less if markets and own-brand products do the heavy lifting. The €40 Navegante transport pass covers metro, buses, trams, and even the ferries — a car is an expense Lisbon actively punishes. Eating out is the classic budget leak: a €12 prato do dia is honest value, but tourist-zone dinners at €25–40 quietly turn "food" into your biggest category — the [discretionary spending](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) trap in azulejo tiles.

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Lisbon?

About €1,750–2,150/month for one person including rent in 2026 — the range depends almost entirely on where the apartment is. Couples sharing a one-bedroom land around €2,400–2,900.

### Is Lisbon still cheap in 2026?

Day-to-day life, yes — groceries, transport, and casual eating out remain well below Paris or Amsterdam levels. Rent, no: a decade of demand has pushed center one-bedrooms to €1,200–1,500, which is the whole reason budgets break.

### Can you live in Lisbon on €1,500 a month?

Yes, with a studio or a room outside the center, groceries over restaurants, and the €40 transport pass. It's a disciplined budget, not a comfortable one — set [category limits](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit) on eating out and it holds.

### What salary do you need for Lisbon as a remote worker?

Net €2,500+/month gives genuine comfort including savings; the [emergency fund calculator](/tools/emergency-fund-calculator) will tell you how much buffer to keep given Lisbon's rising rents.
