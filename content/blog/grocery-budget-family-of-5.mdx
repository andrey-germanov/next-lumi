**A family of five's grocery budget runs roughly $1,200/month on the USDA Thrifty tier up to about $1,950 on the Liberal tier in 2026 — figures that scale the [official family-of-4 plans](/blog/grocery-budget-family-of-4) up by the USDA's own per-person additions.** At five people, groceries are usually the second-largest budget line in the house. Here's where your number should land.

## The benchmarks for a family of 5

| Plan | Monthly cost (approx.) | Weekly |
|------|------------------------|--------|
| Thrifty | ~$1,200 | ~$275 |
| Low-Cost | ~$1,320 | ~$305 |
| Moderate | ~$1,630 | ~$375 |
| Liberal | ~$1,950 | ~$450 |

Derived from [USDA Food Plans](https://www.fna.usda.gov/research/cnpp/usda-food-plans/cost-food-monthly-reports) (updated monthly; the USDA publishes per-person costs by age — add your actual kids' ages for a precise figure). The single biggest modifier: **teenagers**. USDA prices a 14–18-year-old boy near adult-male levels — a family of five with two teens eats like a family of six. Real-world reports agree: [$1,000–1,500/month is the commonly reported range](https://www.ramseysolutions.com/budgeting/average-cost-of-groceries) for disciplined five-person households.

## What changes at five people

Five is where bulk buying stops being optional and starts being arithmetic: warehouse-store staples (rice, oats, flour, frozen vegetables, cheese) run 20–40% below supermarket unit prices, and a five-person household actually finishes them before expiry. Double-batch cooking — three dinners cooked, six dinners served — cuts both cost and the weeknight-delivery failure mode. And waste turns expensive: 15% of $1,600 is $240/month, which makes the freezer-inventory habit worth more than any coupon. ## Keeping the number honest

Five-person grocery spending fragments — two parents, four stores, Costco runs that are one-third non-food — so the "grocery" total in your head is reliably $200+ low. One tracked month gives you the real number: [Lumi](/) auto-imports [card taps](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending), takes [voice logging](/blog/how-to-log-expenses-without-typing) in the parking lot ("Aldi one ninety"), and receipt-scans the rest, with both parents feeding one [shared picture](/blog/what-is-a-shared-budget). Then pick your target tier from the table and treat it as a [weekly spending limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit) — weekly caps beat monthly ones at this transaction volume.

## FAQ

**Is $1,400 a month normal for a family of 5?** Yes — between Low-Cost and Moderate tiers, the national middle for five. With two teens, it's effectively Low-Cost.

**What's a realistic weekly budget for 5?** $280–380 for most families. Under $275 requires Thrifty-tier planning; $450+ is Liberal-tier defaults compounding.

**How much more does the fifth person add?** Roughly 20–22% over a family of four per USDA math — $200–330/month depending on tier and, decisively, on ages. A [family of six adds another similar step](/blog/grocery-budget-family-of-6).
