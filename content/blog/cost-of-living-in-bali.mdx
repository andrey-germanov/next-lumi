## Villas: the 18% problem

Canggu asking rents hit 12–18 million IDR for a one-bedroom pool villa in 2026, up from 10–15 million a year earlier, with owners now demanding two-month deposits. The escape valves: Pererenan and Kedungu (same coast, pre-hype prices), Ubud (greener, cheaper, quieter), or co-living from 5 million. Book monthly, never yearly, until you've lived a wet season.

## The two food economies

A warung plate of nasi campur costs $1–3; the smoothie-bowl-and-flat-white circuit runs $700+/month on exactly the same island. Nobody decides to overspend — it accumulates one $8 breakfast at a time, the textbook [discretionary spending](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) drift. Most of it is cash or QR payments that vanish from memory by evening; [tracking cash spending](/blog/how-to-track-cash-spending) is where Bali budgets get honest, and a voice log ("nasi goreng thirty five thousand") takes two seconds at the counter.

## FAQ

### How much does it cost to live in Bali per month?

$900–1,500 for most nomads at a local-leaning pace; $1,500–2,500 for the comfortable Canggu villa lifestyle; $3,500+ for premium. Rent choice and food defaults drive the spread.

### Is Bali still cheap in 2026?

The island is; Canggu increasingly isn't — villa rents rose 18% year-over-year. Warung food, scooters, and non-hotspot housing remain excellent value.

### How much is a villa in Canggu?

12–18 million IDR (~$750–1,130)/month for a one-bedroom with pool in 2026, with two-month deposits now common. Pererenan, Kedungu, and Ubud undercut it significantly.

### What do nomads forget to budget in Bali?

Visa runs, scooter repairs, flights, and the Western-food drift. The first three are predictable — reserve monthly for them; the last one is visible only if you track it as its own category with a [limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit).
