**Lumi and Monefy solve the same problem — tracking spending without handing over a bank login — but with different ceilings: Monefy makes tapping in an expense fast, Lumi removes the tapping with voice input, Apple Pay auto-import, and receipt scanning.** If you're on Android, Monefy is the pick by default (Lumi is iOS-only). On iPhone, the comparison gets interesting.

## Quick comparison

| | Lumi | Monefy |
|---|------|--------|
| Entry methods | Voice ("coffee 5 euros"), Apple Pay auto-import, receipt scan, manual | Manual one-tap entry, built-in calculator |
| AI | Parses speech, scans receipts, month-end forecast | None |
| Bank login | No | No |
| Where data lives | On your device | Device + optional Google Drive/Dropbox sync |
| Platforms | iOS | iOS + Android |
| Budgets | Category limits with live progress, alerts before overspend | Single budget mode |
| Multi-currency | 150+ with live conversion | Supported, simpler handling |
| Price | Free / $4.99/mo ($39.99/yr) | Free with ads-free paid Premium (regional pricing; custom categories, passcode, sync are paid) |

## Where Monefy wins

Monefy's interface is one of the best pure-manual designs ever shipped: open app, tap category, type amount, done — plus Android support and cross-device sync through your own Google Drive or Dropbox. If you enjoy the small ritual of logging and want the widest platform coverage, Monefy has earned its reputation.

## Where Lumi wins

Manual entry — even great manual entry — is still the step where tracking habits die. Lumi attacks the step itself: say the purchase out loud and AI fills amount, merchant, and category; contactless payments [log themselves via an Apple Pay Shortcut](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending); paper receipts scan in 2–3 seconds. Add category budgets that warn *before* you overspend and an [AI forecast](/blog/how-to-stop-overspending) that projects your month-end balance, and the difference isn't the interface — it's how much of the work disappears.

## Which one should you pick?

Android user: Monefy. iPhone user who likes tapping and wants the simplest possible tool: Monefy is a fine choice. iPhone user who wants tracking to survive busy weeks: Lumi — the entry methods are the product. Both keep your bank credentials out of it, which puts either ahead of sync-based apps on privacy; see [the best expense trackers with no bank login](/blog/best-expense-trackers-no-bank-login-2026) for that full landscape.

## FAQ

### Is Monefy free?

The core app is free. Custom categories, passcode protection, cloud sync, and other extras require the paid Premium, with pricing that varies by region.

### Does either app need my bank account?

No — that's the shared philosophy. Both are manual-first trackers; Lumi adds automation (voice, Apple Pay import, receipt scan) without touching bank credentials.

### Can I import my Monefy history into Lumi?

Monefy exports CSV. Lumi doesn't do bulk CSV import; most switchers start fresh and keep the Monefy export as an archive — spending patterns from a new month arrive quickly.

### Which is better for travel?

Lumi: 150+ currencies with real-time conversion to your home currency, and voice or scan entry works in [multiple languages and currencies on the road](/blog/how-to-track-multiple-currencies).
