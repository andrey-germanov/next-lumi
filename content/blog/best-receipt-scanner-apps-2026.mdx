Manual expense entry is where good intentions go to die. You keep the receipt, mean to log it later, and by the time you remember, the paper is a faded ball in your pocket and you have no idea what the coffee cost. A good receipt scanner app closes that gap. You point your camera, and the amount, date, merchant, and category land in your tracker before you have even put your wallet away.

What changed recently is the technology doing the reading. Old receipt scanners relied on traditional OCR, which matched pixel shapes to characters and fell apart on crumpled paper, thermal receipts, faded ink, or anything not in English. The 2026 generation uses AI vision models that understand a receipt the way a person does, reading context rather than isolated characters. That means fewer corrections, support for many languages, and the ability to pull out a sensible spending category instead of just a total. Below are the receipt scanner apps worth your time this year, and which type of user each one actually fits.

### Lumi — Best receipt scanner for personal spending

Lumi is an iOS app built around a simple idea: tracking money should take seconds, not minutes. Its AI receipt scanning reads the amount, date, merchant, and category from a photo in seconds, and it works in essentially any language, so a receipt from a trip abroad is handled the same as one from your corner store.

Scanning is only one of several ways Lumi captures spending, which is what sets it apart. You can use Back Tap to log a purchase by tapping the back of your phone, let Apple Pay transactions import automatically, or add an expense by voice. Everything flows into one running view of your spending rather than a separate receipt silo. There is no bank login required, so you are not handing over banking credentials to a third party. Lumi supports 150-plus currencies with automatic conversion, and an AI forecast projects where your spending is heading before the month is over.

**Scanning quality:** AI vision reads amount, date, merchant, and category in seconds, in any language.

**Pricing:** Free plan includes 10 AI scans per month. Premium is 4.99 dollars per month for unlimited scans and the full feature set.

**Pros:** Fast multi-method capture, no bank login, strong multi-currency support, private by design.

**Cons:** iOS only, and built for personal budgeting rather than formal business expense reports.

Lumi is the pick if you want to understand and control your own spending. You can [start with Lumi here](/), and it pairs well with a repeatable system like the one in our guide on [how to track spending that actually sticks](/blog/how-to-track-spending-that-actually-sticks).

### Expensify — Best for business expense reports

Expensify is the heavyweight for company expense reporting. Its SmartScan feature reads receipts, and the workflow around it, automatic report generation, approvals, reimbursement, and corporate cards, is built for teams and finance departments rather than individuals.

**Scanning quality:** SmartScan combines OCR with AI and handles crumpled, faded, and multi-language receipts well. Expensify advertises high accuracy across dozens of languages.

**Pricing:** There is a free tier, but it caps SmartScans (users report a small monthly allowance before charges apply). The Collect plan starts at 5 dollars per user per month for unlimited scans, and the Control plan runs 9 dollars per user per month with enterprise features.

**Pros:** Unlimited scanning on paid plans, deep accounting integrations, mature approval and reimbursement flow.

**Cons:** Overkill and pricey for personal use; the free tier's scan limit is tight. This is a business tool.

### Zoho Expense — Best for small-business travel

Zoho Expense targets growing businesses, especially ones with travel and multi-step approvals. Its autoscan reads receipts and turns them into expense entries, and it slots into the wider Zoho ecosystem plus QuickBooks and Xero.

**Scanning quality:** Autoscan OCR is reliable for standard receipts and supports multiple currencies.

**Pricing:** A free plan covers up to 3 users with 20 receipt autoscans total per month. The Standard plan starts around 4 dollars per user per month, and Premium around 7 dollars per user per month, each adding roughly 20 autoscans per user monthly plus card reconciliation and advanced workflows.

**Pros:** Generous free tier for tiny teams, good travel and approval features, strong integrations.

**Cons:** Scan allowances are modest, and it is aimed at business expenses rather than personal budgeting.

### Smart Receipts — Best free and open-source option

Smart Receipts is an open-source, offline-first receipt tracker focused on expense reports. It runs on iOS, Android, and web, and its standout trait is that your data stays on your device by default. It exports polished reports as PDF, CSV, or ZIP files with the receipt images attached.

**Scanning quality:** AI-powered OCR extracts merchant, date, total, tax, and payment method, with more than 20 configurable data fields for reports.

**Pricing:** Free to start with core scanning and tracking. A premium subscription unlocks advanced reporting and automatic cloud backup.

**Pros:** Works offline, open-source and transparent, excellent export options, cross-platform.

**Cons:** The interface is utilitarian, and the report-first design is more about documentation than budgeting insight.

### Veryfi — Best for developers and high-volume capture

Veryfi is really an OCR platform with a consumer-facing app on top. It is the right call if you need to process large volumes of receipts or embed receipt reading into your own software, thanks to a well-regarded API backed by proprietary AI models trained on hundreds of millions of documents.

**Scanning quality:** Among the most accurate available, handling handwriting, tips, taxes, and logo-based vendor recognition across many regions.

**Pricing:** The receipt scanner app has a free tier for basic use. Serious use runs through the API, billed per document processed rather than a flat consumer subscription.

**Pros:** Excellent accuracy, strong compliance credentials, developer-friendly API.

**Cons:** Usage-based pricing gets expensive for casual users, and the value is really in the API, not personal budgeting.

### Foreceipt — Best for tax-time recordkeeping

Foreceipt is built around one goal: making year-end taxes painless for individuals and small businesses in the US and Canada. It scans and categorizes receipts against IRS and CRA categories, captures emailed receipts, and keeps a clean audit trail across mobile and web.

**Scanning quality:** AI-powered OCR with automatic, tax-aware categorization.

**Pricing:** A free plan covers a limited number of receipts with 12-month retention. The individual plan is 6.99 dollars per month or 59.99 dollars per year, and business team pricing starts around 6 dollars per user after a free trial.

**Pros:** Tax-report generation, IRS and CRA category mapping, email and bank-statement capture, all currencies.

**Cons:** The tax focus is North America-centric, and it is less useful if you just want everyday budgeting.

### Finny — Best lightweight personal logger

Finny is an iPhone app in the same personal-tracking lane as Lumi, emphasizing speed and multiple capture methods. It offers batch scanning of several receipt photos at once, a share extension to send images in from other apps, and tap-to-track for Apple Pay, all feeding one unified budget.

**Scanning quality:** AI scanning pulls merchant, amount, date, and category, and processes several receipts in a batch. Multi-currency receipts are kept in their original currency while your totals stay in your default.

**Pricing:** Full expense tracking is free. Finny Pro adds AI features for 1.99 dollars per month or 17.99 dollars per year, with a daily allowance of AI requests.

**Pros:** Inexpensive, fast multi-method capture, offline-friendly, unified personal view.

**Cons:** iOS only, and, like all personal apps here, not designed for company expense reports.

## How to choose

Start by deciding whether you are tracking personal spending or filing business expense reports, because that split matters more than any single feature.

If the goal is personal budgeting, you want a receipt scanner app that turns a photo into a categorized expense with no friction and shows you where your money is going. Prioritize scanning speed, multi-currency support if you travel, privacy (does it demand a bank login?), and a fair free tier. **Lumi** and Finny fit this profile best, with Lumi adding forecasting and multiple no-typing capture methods.

If the goal is business expense reports, you need approvals, reimbursement, accounting integrations, and per-user administration. Expensify and Zoho Expense are built for that, and Foreceipt bridges toward tax documentation. For developers or anyone processing thousands of receipts, Veryfi's API is the specialist choice. And if you value offline control and open-source transparency, Smart Receipts stands alone.

Whatever you pick, the tool only works if you actually use it, so choose the one whose scanning flow you will not avoid. If you are pairing a scanner with a broader plan, our walkthrough on [how to budget](/blog/how-to-budget) covers the structure that receipt data plugs into.

## FAQ

**Are AI receipt scanners more accurate than old OCR apps?**
Generally yes. Traditional OCR reads character shapes and struggles with faded thermal paper, crumpled receipts, and non-English text. AI vision models read a receipt in context, which improves accuracy, category detection, and language coverage. You will still want to glance at scans occasionally, but corrections are far less frequent.

**Do receipt scanner apps need access to my bank account?**
It depends on the app. Business tools often connect to cards and bank feeds for reconciliation. Personal apps like Lumi deliberately skip bank logins, so you scan or capture spending yourself and nothing is pulled from your accounts, which keeps your banking credentials private.

**Which is best for tracking personal spending rather than expense reports?**
For personal budgeting on an iPhone, Lumi is the strongest fit: AI receipt scanning in seconds, 150-plus currencies, no bank login, an AI forecast, and a free plan with 10 scans per month before its 4.99 dollars per month Premium tier. Finny is a solid, cheaper alternative if you want lightweight logging.
