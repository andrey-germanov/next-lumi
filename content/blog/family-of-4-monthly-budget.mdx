**A typical US family of four spends roughly $6,000–9,000 per month in 2026 — housing at ~30%, groceries between $993 (USDA Thrifty) and $1,627 (Liberal), transportation near $1,200, with childcare as the swing variable that can add $2,000+ or nothing at all.** Here are the averages and a template built on them.

## Family of 4 monthly budget: the averages

| Category | Monthly range | Notes |
|----------|--------------|-------|
| Housing (mortgage/rent + utilities) | $2,000–2,900 | ~30% of spending nationally |
| Groceries | $993–1,627 | [USDA food plans](https://www.fna.usda.gov/research/cnpp/usda-food-plans/cost-food-monthly-reports), family of 4 |
| Transportation (usually 2 cars) | $1,000–1,400 | payments, insurance, gas |
| Healthcare & insurance | $600–900 | employer plan assumed |
| Childcare / school costs | $0–2,500+ | the wild card — see below |
| Eating out & entertainment | $400–700 | |
| Clothing, personal, misc | $350–550 | |
| **Total** | **~$6,000–9,000** | before savings |

Benchmarks: [average household expenses](/blog/average-monthly-expenses-2026); location moves everything — the same family runs [$5,500–8,000 in Houston](/blog/cost-of-living-in-houston) and [$9,000–14,000 in NYC](/blog/cost-of-living-in-new-york-city).

## The two numbers that decide the budget

**Childcare** is binary: two kids in full-time care can cost $2,000–4,500/month — rivaling the mortgage — then drops to school-age numbers almost overnight. Budget the expensive years as a phase with an end date, and pre-decide where the freed money goes when it ends (hint: [the 35 checkpoint](/blog/how-much-savings-by-35) is usually waiting). **Groceries** are the controllable one: the USDA's own Thrifty-to-Liberal spread is $634/month for the identical family — meal planning and store choice are worth more than any subscription audit. Full breakdown: [grocery budget for a family of 4](/blog/grocery-budget-family-of-4).

## A template that survives real life

Classic [50/30/20](/blog/what-is-the-50-30-20-rule) strains with kids — needs often run 55–65%, and that's structure, not failure. What matters: savings automated first ([pay yourself first](/blog/what-is-pay-yourself-first), even if 10%), [sinking funds](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund) for the predictable irregulars (school-year startup, sports, holidays, car repairs — a family of four has *many*), and both parents seeing the same numbers ([shared budget](/blog/what-is-a-shared-budget), [right app](/blog/best-budget-apps-for-families-2026)). Four people generate hundreds of transactions a month; the budget only stays true if capture is effortless — [voice logging and automatic Apple Pay import](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending) in [Lumi](/) exist for exactly this volume.

## FAQ

**What's a good income for a family of four?** Against a $6,000–9,000 spend, $85–120k covers median-cost metros with a real savings rate; high-cost coasts push the comfortable line past $150k.

**How much should a family of 4 save monthly?** Target 15–20% of take-home; during peak-childcare years, protect *any* automated rate and restore the full rate the month care ends.

**Biggest family-of-4 budget mistake?** Letting the childcare-graduation windfall dissolve into [lifestyle creep](/blog/what-is-lifestyle-creep) — it's the single largest planned windfall most families ever get.
