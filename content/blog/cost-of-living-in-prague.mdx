**A realistic all-in budget for a single person in Prague runs 30,000–45,000 Kč per month (~$1,300–2,000) in 2026 — roughly $770 of daily costs plus 13,000–28,000 Kč rent depending on district.** Prague remains the rare European capital where beauty and affordability still overlap. Here's the breakdown.

## Prague monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (CZK) |
|----------|--------------------|
| 1-bedroom, central districts | 18,000–28,000 |
| 1-bedroom, outside center | 13,000–22,000 |
| Groceries (Lidl, Billa, Albert, Kaufland) | 5,000–8,000 |
| Utilities (more in winter) | 4,000–7,500 |
| Transit pass (unlimited) | 550 |
| Daily costs ex-rent | ~18,000 (~$770) |
| **Realistic total** | **30,000–45,000 Kč (~$1,300–2,000)** |

Sources: [Numbeo Prague](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Prague), [Expatistan](https://www.expatistan.com/cost-of-living/prague) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: the district gradient

Vinohrady and Letná carry the expat premium at 22,000–28,000 Kč for a one-bedroom; Žižkov next door does the same life at 18,000; Prague 9 and 10 drop to 13,000–16,000 with a 20-minute tram ride. The 550 Kč unlimited transit pass — one of Europe's great bargains — makes distance nearly free, which is why chasing a central address is Prague's most common budget mistake.

## Daily life: beer-economy math

Groceries at 5,000–8,000 Kč, lunch menus at 180–250 Kč, and the famous sub-60-Kč pub beer keep daily life gentle. The leaks are winter utilities (heating swings the 4,000–7,500 Kč range hard — budget the top and bank the difference) and the tourist-zone price gradient: the same beer costs 3x two blocks from Old Town Square. Expats who eat like locals bank the difference; those who drift into the center's [discretionary economy](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) don't.

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Prague?

30,000–45,000 Kč/month (~$1,300–2,000) all-in for a single person in 2026. Frugal setups run lower; comfort with regular dining out sits at the top of the range.

### Is Prague still cheap in 2026?

Cheaper than Western Europe, no longer "cheap" absolutely — rents have climbed steadily. Daily costs (transit, beer, groceries, lunch menus) remain among the EU's best values.

### How much is rent in Prague?

18,000–28,000 Kč for a central one-bedroom, 13,000–22,000 Kč outside the center. Žižkov is the classic value pick; the transit pass makes outer districts painless.

### What surprises expats in Prague's budget?

Winter heating (the 4,000–7,500 Kč utility spread is seasonal), the tourist-zone markup, and Czech annual billing reconciliations. Budget utilities high, drink beer where locals do, and track the difference.
