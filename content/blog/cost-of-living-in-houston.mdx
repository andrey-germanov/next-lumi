**A realistic all-in budget for a single person in Houston runs $2,100–3,000 per month in 2026 — the cheapest big-city rent in America (median one-bedroom ~$1,068, with 19.5% vacancy keeping it soft), offset by the two costs Houston never waives: a car and air conditioning.** Here's the honest math.

## Houston monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (USD) |
|----------|--------------------|
| 1-bedroom, Montrose/Heights/Midtown | $1,100–1,500 |
| 1-bedroom, outer loops/suburbs | $850–1,200 |
| Car: payment, insurance, gas | $600–800 |
| Groceries (single person) | $350–500 |
| Utilities + internet (summer AC heavy) | $180–300 |
| Eating out (moderate) | $250–400 |
| Daily costs ex-rent (incl. car) | ~$1,500 |
| **Realistic total** | **$2,100–3,000** |

Sources: [ApartmentAdvisor](https://www.apartmentadvisor.com/national-rent-report), [Apartments.com rent report](https://www.apartments.com/blog/apartments.com-national-rent-trends-report-january-2026), [Numbeo](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Houston) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: a buyer's market for renters

19.5% vacancy — the highest among major US metros — means Houston landlords compete for *you*: concessions, free months, and negotiable renewals are standard. Montrose and the Heights carry the walkable premium at $1,100–1,500; the sprawl beyond the loops drops under $1,000. Negotiate everything — in this market, not asking is a $100–200/month mistake.

## The two non-negotiables: car and AC

Houston is America's most car-mandatory big city: $600–800/month all-in, no realistic opt-out. Summer electricity is the second fixed cost — six months of serious AC push July–September bills to $250–350. Budget both like rent. The upside: no state income tax, groceries among the cheapest of any major metro, and a restaurant scene where $12 still buys a remarkable plate. Cheap rent plus expensive car is Houston's whole equation — run it honestly before comparing with transit cities like [Chicago](/blog/cost-of-living-in-chicago).

## Cost of living in Houston for a single person vs a family of 4

Single: $2,100–3,000. Family of four: 3BR house at $1,600–2,400, two cars ($1,200–1,600), $900–1,200 groceries, childcare $900–1,400 per child — totals $5,500–8,000, among the lowest of any major US metro and the reason Houston keeps absorbing families. ## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Houston?

$2,100–3,000/month all-in for a single person in 2026; sub-$1,900 is achievable with roommates in a 19.5%-vacancy market.

### What salary do you need for Houston?

$55–75k covers a comfortable solo setup including the car; no state income tax stretches it further.

### Is Houston the cheapest big city in America?

By median one-bedroom rent among the largest metros — essentially yes. Add mandatory car costs and it's still near the bottom of total-budget tables.

### What surprises people about Houston costs?

Summer electric bills, car insurance, toll roads — and how much negotiating power renters have right now.
