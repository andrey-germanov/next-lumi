**A realistic all-in budget for a single person in Denver runs $2,700–3,700 per month in 2026 — a median one-bedroom near $1,453, ~$1,350 of daily costs, and the city's signature line item: the mountain weekend.** Denver sits almost exactly at the national midpoint on rent while charging coastal prices for fun. Here's the breakdown.

## Denver monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (USD) |
|----------|--------------------|
| 1-bedroom, LoDo/Cap Hill/Highlands | $1,400–1,900 |
| 1-bedroom, Aurora/Lakewood/outer | $1,100–1,400 |
| Car: payment, insurance, gas | $550–750 |
| Groceries (single person) | $400–550 |
| Utilities + internet | $130–200 |
| RTD transit pass | $88 |
| Eating out + going out (moderate) | $250–450 |
| Daily costs ex-rent (incl. car) | ~$1,500 |
| **Realistic total** | **$2,700–3,700** |

Sources: [ApartmentAdvisor](https://www.apartmentadvisor.com/national-rent-report), [Numbeo](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Denver), [RentCafe](https://www.rentcafe.com/average-rent-market-trends/us/) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: mid-table and stabilizing

Denver's post-boom construction cooled the market: $1,400–1,900 central, $1,100–1,400 in Aurora or Lakewood on the RTD lines. The $88 regional pass covers light rail to most job centers, but Denver life leans car-shaped the moment the mountains enter the plan — which for most residents is the point of being here.

## The real budget: the mountains

Denver's discretionary spending has a shape no national average captures: ski passes ($400–1,000/season), gear that accumulates like [lifestyle creep](/blog/what-is-lifestyle-creep) with carabiners, gas for I-70 weekends, and the après economy. It's joy, not leakage — but it belongs in the budget as a named [sinking fund](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund) (pass + gear + trips ÷ 12), not as a monthly surprise. Treat it like [conscious spending](/blog/what-is-conscious-spending): planned, capped, guilt-free.

## Cost of living in Denver for a single person vs a family of 4

Single: $2,700–3,700 with a car and a modest mountain habit. Family of four: 3BR at $2,200–3,200, two cars, $1,000–1,300 groceries, childcare $1,200–1,700 per child — totals $6,500–9,000. ## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Denver?

$2,700–3,700/month all-in for a single person in 2026; $2,300–2,600 with roommates and a restrained gear habit.

### What salary is comfortable in Denver?

$70–90k covers solo comfort including the mountain budget; Colorado's flat income tax keeps the math simple.

### Is Denver expensive?

Mid-table nationally on rent — well under coastal cities, above most of Texas. The lifestyle spending is where Denver budgets diverge from the averages.

### What surprises people about Denver costs?

The all-in cost of the outdoor life, I-70 weekend gas and food, and how fast ski-season spending compounds without a named fund.
