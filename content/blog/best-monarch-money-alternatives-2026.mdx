Monarch Money earned its spot as the default Mint replacement: clean design, solid budgets, shared access for couples, and reliable bank sync. But at 14.99 dollars a month (or 99.99 dollars a year), it's one of the most expensive budgeting apps on the market — and its whole model depends on linking your bank accounts through an aggregator. If either the price or the bank login is why you're here, these are the honest alternatives.

## Why people leave Monarch

Three reasons come up again and again. Price: 100 dollars a year is real money for a budgeting tool, especially if you use a fraction of its features. Bank linking: some people can't (unsupported banks, cash-heavy spending) and some won't (handing an aggregator read access to every account is a privacy decision, not a technicality). And overkill: if you mainly want to know where your money goes each month, a full wealth-management dashboard is more app than the job requires.

## The alternatives, by what you actually want

### Lumi — if you want privacy and speed instead of bank sync

[Lumi](/) flips Monarch's model: no bank login at all, data stays on your device, and logging is fast enough that manual doesn't mean tedious. Say "groceries forty dollars" and AI logs and categorizes it; Apple Pay purchases import automatically via a Shortcut; receipts scan in seconds. You get category budgets, savings goals, an AI month-end forecast, and 150+ currencies — free to start, with Premium at 4.99 dollars a month, a third of Monarch's price. The trade-off is honest: no automatic net-worth dashboard pulled from linked accounts. What you get instead is awareness — you notice what you spend because logging takes two seconds, not zero.

### YNAB — if you want a method, not just a dashboard

YNAB is a budgeting philosophy (zero-based, every dollar gets a job) with software attached. At 109 dollars a year it's pricier than Monarch, but people who click with the method swear by it for decades. People who don't click with it churn in a month. Know which you are before paying.

### Rocket Money — if subscriptions are your main leak

Rocket Money's core value is finding and cancelling recurring charges, with budgeting attached. The free tier is limited and Premium uses choose-your-price billing (roughly 7–14 dollars a month). If forgotten subscriptions are the actual problem, though, you can also fix that yourself in an hour — our [guide to finding forgotten subscriptions](/blog/how-to-find-forgotten-subscriptions) walks through every hiding place, and the [subscription cost calculator](/tools/subscription-cost-calculator) puts a yearly number on the damage.

### Copilot — if you want Monarch's polish, iPhone-only

Copilot matches Monarch on design quality and undercuts it slightly on price, with excellent transaction categorization. It's iPhone/Mac only and still bank-sync based — so it solves the price complaint, not the privacy one.

### PocketGuard — if you want cheap and simple

PocketGuard's "in my pocket" number (what's safe to spend after bills and goals) is genuinely useful, and it's one of the cheaper sync-based options. Less depth than Monarch everywhere else — which for many people is the point.

## The bottom line

Leaving over price: PocketGuard or Lumi. Leaving over bank linking: Lumi is the only one on this list built entirely without it. Wanting more method: YNAB. Wanting the same experience cheaper on iPhone: Copilot. Chasing subscription leaks: Rocket Money, or the DIY route plus a tracker that reminds you before renewals.

## FAQ

### Is Monarch Money worth $100 a year?

If you actively use bank sync across many accounts, shared couple access, and investment tracking — it can be. If you mostly check category spending, you're paying wealth-dashboard prices for a budget tracker, and cheaper or private alternatives cover that job.

### What's the best Monarch alternative without bank linking?

Lumi is built for exactly that model: voice input, Apple Pay auto-import via Shortcut, and receipt scanning replace the sync, so no aggregator ever sees your accounts. Data stays on your device.

### Can I export my Monarch data before switching?

Yes — Monarch supports CSV export of transactions. Most alternatives (including Lumi via manual review, and YNAB via import tools) let you start from that history, though many people treat a switch as a clean start instead.
