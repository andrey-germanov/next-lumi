**Toshl and Lumi are two of the strongest multi-currency expense trackers, and they split the audience cleanly: Toshl for people who want maximum currency coverage (≈200 currencies including crypto) and quirky character, Lumi for people who want entries to log themselves — by voice, Apple Pay import, or receipt scan.** Here's the honest split.

## Quick comparison

| | Lumi | Toshl |
|---|------|-------|
| Currencies | 150+, live conversion to home currency | ~200 incl. ~30 cryptocurrencies, per-item currency |
| Entry methods | Voice, Apple Pay auto-import, receipt scan, manual | Manual (fast forms), bank import on Medici (US/CA banks) |
| AI | Speech parsing, receipt vision, month-end forecast | None (character-based charm instead) |
| Bank login | Never needed | Optional, Medici plan, US/Canada |
| Where data lives | On your device | Toshl cloud |
| Platforms | iOS | iOS, Android, web |
| Paid price | $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr | Medici $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr; cheaper Pro below it |

## Where Toshl wins

Currency coverage is the widest in the category — roughly 200 currencies plus crypto, with the ability to set currency per item, which matters if a single day includes euros, forints, and Bitcoin. The web app and Android support make it usable everywhere, and Toshl's monster-illustrated personality has kept people logging for a decade. If your finances span US/Canadian banks, the Medici plan adds automatic import.

## Where Lumi wins

The entry itself. Toshl's forms are quick, but they're still forms — and on a travel day with twelve small purchases, forms lose to saying ["taxi twelve euros"](/blog/how-to-log-expenses-without-typing) or letting [Apple Pay tap-payments import themselves](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending). Receipt scanning reads foreign-language receipts — the exact artifact travel generates in bulk. Same yearly price as Toshl Medici ($39.99), but the automation comes without connecting any bank, and data stays on the device rather than in a cloud account.

## Which one should you pick?

Android or web-first, crypto in the mix, per-item currency juggling: Toshl. iPhone traveler or expat who wants the day's spending captured with near-zero effort: Lumi. Either beats salary-country apps that treat foreign currency as an afterthought — the wider list is in [best multi-currency expense trackers](/blog/best-multi-currency-expense-tracker-2026), and the daily mechanics in [how to track multiple currencies](/blog/how-to-track-multiple-currencies).

## FAQ

### How many currencies does each app support?

Toshl: about 200, including ~30 cryptocurrencies. Lumi: 150+ fiat currencies with real-time conversion (2 currencies on the free plan, all on Premium). If crypto tracking is a requirement, Toshl wins outright.

### Do I need a bank connection with either app?

No for both cores. Toshl's Medici plan optionally imports from US/Canadian banks; Lumi never connects to banks — automation comes from voice, Apple Pay Shortcuts, and receipt scanning instead.

### Which is better for a two-week trip?

The one where logging survives vacation laziness. Voice entry and Apple Pay auto-import mean a Lumi trip log mostly writes itself; Toshl asks for a form per expense — quick, but times fifty purchases it's the difference between a complete record and a half-abandoned one.

### Is Toshl's price comparable to Lumi's?

Feature-equivalent tiers land at the same $39.99/year (Toshl Medici vs Lumi Premium). Toshl also has a cheaper Pro tier without bank import; Lumi has a free tier with voice input and 10 AI scans a month.
