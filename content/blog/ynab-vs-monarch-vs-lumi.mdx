## What each app is actually for

### YNAB: a method with software attached

YNAB is zero-based budgeting enforced by design: assign every incoming dollar to a category before you spend it, reconcile weekly, roll with the punches when categories overspend. People who adopt the method keep it for decades; people who don't churn within a month. At $109/yr you're paying for the discipline framework — the software alone isn't the point. If the method intrigues you but the price doesn't, see [zero-based budgeting explained](/blog/what-is-zero-based-budgeting) and the [best YNAB alternatives](/blog/best-ynab-alternatives-2026).

### Monarch: the full-picture dashboard

Monarch links your accounts through an aggregator and assembles the complete picture automatically: spending by category, net worth over time, investments, recurring charges. It's the closest thing to a Mint successor, and shared access makes it strong for couples with joint finances. The trade-offs are structural: ~$100/yr, and the model requires granting read access to every account — a privacy decision, not a technicality. More in [best Monarch alternatives](/blog/best-monarch-money-alternatives-2026).

### Lumi: awareness without the bank login

[Lumi](/) skips the aggregator entirely. Expenses arrive in seconds — say "groceries forty dollars" and AI parses amount, merchant, and category; Apple Pay purchases [import automatically via a Shortcut](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending); receipts scan in 2–3 seconds. Category budgets, savings goals, an AI month-end forecast, and 150+ currencies are built in, and data stays on your device. The honest trade-off: no automatic net-worth dashboard pulled from linked accounts — Lumi optimizes for knowing where your money goes, not for wealth reporting.

## How to choose in one minute

Ask yourself which failure mode you're avoiding. Overspending because money has no plan → YNAB. Not seeing the full picture across many accounts → Monarch. Quitting tracking because logging is tedious, or refusing to hand over bank credentials → Lumi. Price check, per year: YNAB $109, Monarch ~$100, Lumi $39.99 (or free to start).

## FAQ

### Is YNAB or Monarch better?

They solve different problems: YNAB teaches and enforces a budgeting method; Monarch automates a financial overview. YNAB rewards effort, Monarch rewards connection. If you want neither effort-heavy method nor bank linking, that's the gap Lumi fills.

### Which is cheapest?

Lumi: free plan (10 AI scans/month, 2 budgets, voice input, Apple Pay import), Premium at $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr — roughly a third of YNAB ($109/yr) or Monarch (~$100/yr).

### Can I use any of these without linking my bank?

YNAB technically allows manual entry but is built around sync. Monarch requires linking. Lumi is the only one of the three designed entirely without bank access — logging is fast enough (voice, auto-import, scan) that sync isn't missed.

### Which app is best for couples?

Fully joint finances: Monarch (shared access) or YNAB (one shared budget). Separate or hybrid finances: Lumi — each partner tracks privately and you settle shared costs monthly; see [best budget apps for couples](/blog/best-budget-apps-for-couples-2026).
