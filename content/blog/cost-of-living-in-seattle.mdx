**A realistic all-in budget for a single person in Seattle runs $3,000–4,300 per month in 2026 — a median one-bedroom near $1,795 (central neighborhoods $1,800–2,400), roughly $1,400 of daily costs, no state income tax, and grocery prices that lead the nation.** Here's the breakdown.

## Seattle monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (USD) |
|----------|--------------------|
| 1-bedroom, Capitol Hill/Ballard/SLU | $1,800–2,400 |
| 1-bedroom, Northgate/West Seattle/Renton | $1,400–1,800 |
| Groceries (single person) | $500–700 |
| Utilities + internet | $150–230 |
| ORCA transit pass | ~$100 |
| Eating out (moderate) | $300–500 |
| Daily costs ex-rent | ~$1,400 |
| **Realistic total** | **$3,000–4,300** |

Sources: [ApartmentAdvisor](https://www.apartmentadvisor.com/national-rent-report), [Numbeo](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Seattle), [Zumper](https://www.zumper.com/rent-research/national-rent-report) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: the light-rail dividend

Capitol Hill and Ballard carry the walkability premium at $1,800–2,400; the Link light rail's expansion made Northgate, Columbia City, and even suburban stops real options at $1,400–1,800 with a predictable commute. Many Seattleites skip the car entirely — the ORCA pass plus occasional rideshare beats $650+/month of ownership, the same trade that powers [Chicago's math](/blog/cost-of-living-in-chicago).

## Daily life: the grocery capital

Seattle routinely tops US grocery-price surveys — $500–700/month for one person is normal, 20–30% over national. Add the coffee ritual (a real line item here) and eating out at tech-city prices, and daily spend quietly outruns expectations even as rent behaves. This is the city where [tracking groceries against a benchmark](/blog/how-much-to-spend-on-groceries) pays for itself fastest. No state income tax gives back $300–500/month at tech salaries.

## Cost of living in Seattle for a single person vs a family of 4

Single: $3,000–4,300, car-optional. Family of four: 3BR at $2,600–3,800, groceries $1,300–1,700 (that grocery premium scales painfully with mouths), childcare $1,400–2,000 per child, usually one car — totals $7,500–10,500. ## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Seattle?

$3,000–4,300/month all-in for a single person in 2026; $2,500–2,800 with roommates or a light-rail suburb.

### What salary is comfortable in Seattle?

$85–110k for a solo one-bedroom life; no state income tax makes gross salaries stretch further than California equivalents.

### Is Seattle cheaper than San Francisco?

Meaningfully — rent runs 40–50% below [SF](/blog/cost-of-living-in-san-francisco), while groceries are comparable or higher. Total solo budgets land $1,000–1,700/month lighter.

### What surprises people about Seattle costs?

Grocery prices leading the nation, the coffee line item, and how much the light rail changed the rent map.
