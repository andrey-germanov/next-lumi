**A single person in Kyiv spends about $1,000–1,200 per month with a central one-bedroom in 2026, and far less — $600–800 — outside the center: daily costs run ~$544 ex-rent, and the transport pass is $11.** Kyiv remains one of Europe's most affordable capitals in hard currency, with a resilient, fully functional city life. Here's the breakdown.

| Category | Monthly cost |

Sources: [Numbeo Kyiv](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Kiev), [Wise](https://wise.com/us/cost-of-living/ukraine/kiev-kyiv) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: the widest spread in Europe

Kyiv's rental market has an extreme center-to-edge gradient: renovated one-bedrooms near Khreshchatyk or in Pechersk average $655, while solid apartments on the left bank or in Obolon start near $130–190. The metro — fast, deep, and practically free — makes the outer districts a genuine option rather than a compromise, which is why local budgets and expat budgets for the same city differ threefold.

## Daily life: hryvnia value

Groceries from Silpo and ATB, market produce, and café culture where a specialty flat white costs $2.30 — daily Kyiv is remarkable value in hard currency. Utilities at ~$99 include heating; the seasonal winter peak deserves a [sinking-fund buffer](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund). Delivery (Glovo, Bolt Food) and the growing restaurant scene are where budgets drift — same [discretionary pattern](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) as everywhere, at gentler prices.
