Rocket Money built its name on one killer feature: it finds your forgotten subscriptions and offers to cancel them for you. That's a real problem solved. But users leave for predictable reasons — the choose-your-price Premium (roughly 7–14 dollars a month) feels steep for what's mostly a subscription list, the budgeting features are thin next to dedicated apps, bill negotiation takes a cut of your savings (typically 30–60%), and everything requires linking your bank accounts. Here's what to use instead, depending on which part you actually need.

## First, an honest question: do you need an app for this at all?

Rocket Money's headline job — finding forgotten subscriptions — is a one-hour DIY task. Check Apple ID subscriptions, Google Play, PayPal automatic payments, and three months of statements, then search your email for "renewal." Our [guide to finding forgotten subscriptions](/blog/how-to-find-forgotten-subscriptions) covers all six hiding places, and the [subscription cost calculator](/tools/subscription-cost-calculator) turns your list into a per-month, per-year, and opportunity-cost number. What you can't DIY is the *ongoing* part — knowing before each renewal — and that's what the alternatives below solve.

## The alternatives

### Lumi — subscription tracking without the bank login

[Lumi](/) keeps all recurring payments in one list and reminds you before each charge — the renewal becomes something you approve, not something you discover. Unlike Rocket Money, it doesn't need your bank credentials to do this: expenses arrive by voice ("Netflix fifteen dollars"), Apple Pay auto-import, or receipt scan, and data stays on your device. Around it you get a full expense tracker — category budgets, AI month-end forecast, savings goals, 150+ currencies. Free to start, Premium 4.99 dollars a month, no percentage cut of anything.

### Monarch Money — if you want the full financial dashboard

If Rocket Money's budgeting felt thin, Monarch is the deep end: proper budgets, net worth, investments, couple access — at 14.99 dollars a month / 99.99 a year, with bank sync at the core. It detects recurring charges too, though cancellation stays your job. See our full breakdown of [Monarch alternatives](/blog/best-monarch-money-alternatives-2026) if you're weighing that direction.

### YNAB — if the real problem is overspending, not subscriptions

Subscriptions are often the visible symptom of a budget that doesn't exist. YNAB's zero-based method (109 dollars a year) forces every dollar to have a job, which surfaces recurring waste automatically — no detection feature needed, because you assigned that money yourself.

### PocketGuard — the budget-lite middle ground

PocketGuard sits between Rocket Money and Monarch: bank-synced, recurring-charge detection, and a simple "in my pocket" spendable number, at a lower price point than either. Good for people who want light-touch automation without Monarch's depth or cost.

### Your bank's own tools — the free baseline

Most major banks now flag recurring charges and let you block card payments to specific merchants. It's crude, it won't remind you before renewals, and it only sees one bank — but it's free and requires trusting no one new.

## The bottom line

Only need the subscription cleanup: do it yourself in an hour, it's the best hourly rate in personal finance. Want ongoing renewal reminders without handing over bank credentials: Lumi. Want deeper budgeting with sync: Monarch or PocketGuard. Want to fix the underlying overspending: YNAB — or start smaller with our guide on [how to track subscriptions](/blog/how-to-track-subscriptions) and a real budget from the [50/30/20 calculator](/tools/50-30-20-budget-calculator).

## FAQ

### Is Rocket Money's bill negotiation worth it?

It can save real money on cable and phone bills, but read the terms: the service typically keeps 30–60% of the first year's savings, charged upfront in some cases. Calling the provider yourself with a competitor's offer achieves the same discount and costs nothing.

### Can Rocket Money cancel any subscription?

No — some merchants require you to cancel directly, and platform subscriptions (Apple, Google) are usually faster to cancel yourself in Settings. The cancellation concierge is convenience, not magic.

### What's the most private way to track subscriptions?

Keep the list in a tracker that doesn't touch your bank: log each subscription once with its renewal date and let the app remind you before charges. Lumi does this on-device with no bank login — the subscription list is yours, not an aggregator's.
