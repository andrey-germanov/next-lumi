**A single person in Bucharest spends about $1,200–2,000 per month including rent in 2026 — roughly 3,500 lei (~$770) of daily costs plus €600–800 for a central one-bedroom — with genuine comfort starting around 8,000–9,000 lei net.** Bucharest remains the EU's most underrated value capital: gigabit internet, EU infrastructure, non-EU prices. Here's the breakdown.

## Rent: doubled in a decade, still a bargain

Central one-bedrooms went from €350–400 to €600–800 since 2016 — Bucharest's own version of the European rent story — yet the same money that gets a room in Barcelona gets a whole renovated apartment near Piața Romană here. The value map: Floreasca and Dorobanți carry the premium; Titan, Drumul Taberei, and Militari halve the rent for a metro commute; and the metro itself costs pennies.

## Daily life: lei math

Careful home cooking covers food and household basics at 1,200–1,600 lei; eating out stays honest (a good lunch at 40–60 lei) outside the Old Town tourist strip. The winter utility spike is the budget's seasonal event — the £151 average hides a cold-month peak, so budget high and bank the difference as a [sinking fund](/blog/what-is-a-sinking-fund). The drift risk is delivery culture: Glovo and Tazz turn 25-lei meals into 60-lei orders, the local face of [discretionary spending](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending).

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Bucharest?

$1,200–2,000/month including rent for a comfortable single life in 2026; genuine comfort with savings starts around 8,000–9,000 lei net.

### Is Bucharest cheap in 2026?

For an EU capital, remarkably — daily costs run half of Western European levels, and rent, though doubled since 2016, still undercuts every comparable capital. Salaries-to-costs, it's one of Europe's best arbitrage cities for remote workers.

### How much is rent in Bucharest?

€600–800 for a central one-bedroom; ~€380 outside the center. Titan and Drumul Taberei are the metro-connected value picks.

### What surprises expats in Bucharest's budget?

The winter heating spike, Old Town tourist pricing, and how fast delivery apps compound. Track utilities monthly against the annual average and the seasonal shock disappears.
