You tapped your phone at the register, the terminal beeped, and now you're wondering where that payment actually went. Apple Pay transaction history exists — but it's scattered across places, and none of them gives you the full picture of your spending. Here's where to look, what each view can and can't show, and how to turn taps into an actual spending record.

## Where to find Apple Pay transactions

### In the Wallet app, per card

Open **Wallet**, tap a card, and you'll see **Latest Transactions** — recent Apple Pay payments made with that card on this device. Tap a transaction for details: merchant, amount, date, and sometimes a map. Two catches: history is shown *per card*, not combined, and what appears depends on your bank — some banks feed rich detail into Wallet, others show little or nothing.

### In Settings, per device

Go to **Settings → Wallet & Apple Pay**, tap a card, and open the **Transactions** tab. Useful for checking what was paid from a specific device — an Apple Watch, for instance, keeps its own list.

### In your bank's app — the source of truth

Every Apple Pay payment is ultimately a card payment, so your bank's app or statement is the complete, authoritative record. If Wallet shows nothing for a card, this is where the money trail lives. The downside: it's mixed with all your other transactions, uncategorized or auto-categorized badly, and split across every bank you use.

## Why Wallet history isn't a spending tracker

Wallet answers "did this payment go through?" — it doesn't answer "how much did I spend on groceries this month?" Four structural gaps: history is fragmented per card and device rather than combined; detail depends on your bank's integration; cash, physical-card swipes, and transfers never appear at all; and there are no categories, budgets, or totals. Checking Wallet tells you about the last tap. It cannot tell you about your month.

## FAQ

### Can I see all my Apple Pay transactions in one list?

Not natively. Wallet shows history per card, per device, with detail depending on your bank. The only combined views are your bank statements (per bank) or an expense tracker that captures each transaction as it happens via the Shortcuts Transaction trigger.

### Why is a transaction missing from Wallet?

Usual suspects: the card's bank doesn't share transaction data with Wallet, the payment was made on another device (Watch, iPad), or it was a physical card swipe rather than Apple Pay. The bank app will have it regardless.

### Does Apple see what I buy with Apple Pay?

Apple states that Apple Pay transactions aren't stored on Apple's servers in a way that's tied to you for marketing. Device-visible history is stored on your device and with your bank — one more reason the bank remains the authoritative record.
