**Buddy and Lumi are both iPhone-native money apps, but they start from opposite ends: Buddy starts with the plan (set a budget, invite a partner, watch the envelopes), Lumi starts with the data (capture every expense in seconds, then budget from reality).** Planners love Buddy. People whose plans die at the data-entry step tend to end up on Lumi.

## Quick comparison

| | Lumi | Buddy |
|---|------|-------|
| Core idea | Effortless capture: voice, Apple Pay import, receipt scan | Friendly budget planning, envelopes-style |
| Entry methods | Voice, auto-import, scan, manual | Manual; bank import in supported regions |
| Shared budgets | No — each tracks own, settle up monthly | Yes, invite partner/friends, split expenses |
| AI | Speech parsing, receipt vision, month-end forecast | None |
| Bank login | Never | Optional, region-dependent |
| Where data lives | On your device | Cloud (required for shared budgets) |
| Free tier | Voice, Apple Pay import, 10 scans/mo, 2 budgets | Limited free version |
| Paid price | $4.99/mo or $39.99/yr | $9.99/mo or $49.99/yr |

## Where Buddy wins

Buddy's onboarding is one of the friendliest ways to *start* budgeting on iPhone: pick categories, set amounts, and the app's cheerful design makes the plan feel achievable. Shared budgets are the standout — invite a partner or roommate, split expenses, and see who paid what, all in one place. If your goal is a collaborative plan you build together, Buddy does that job directly.

## Where Lumi wins

A budget is only as good as the data underneath it, and manual entry is where the data stops arriving. Lumi's answer is capture-first: say ["groceries forty dollars"](/blog/how-to-log-expenses-without-typing), let [Apple Pay purchases import themselves](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending), scan the receipt when there's paper. Budgets then run on complete data — with live progress bars, warnings *before* the limit, and an [AI forecast](/blog/how-to-stop-overspending) projecting your month-end balance. It's also cheaper ($39.99 vs $49.99/yr), and nothing requires a cloud account or bank access.

## Which one should you pick?

Roommates or a couple building one shared plan together: Buddy — that's its home turf. An individual (or a separate-finances couple) who wants tracking that survives busy weeks: Lumi. For the shared-money question specifically, shared budgets aren't the only model — [separate tracking with a monthly settle-up](/blog/how-to-budget-as-a-couple-separate-accounts) often outlives shared databases, and the [couple expense split calculator](/tools/couple-expense-split-calculator) does the math.

## FAQ

### Is Buddy free?

There's a limited free version; Premium unlocks the full feature set at $9.99/month or $49.99/year — currently the pricier of the two apps compared here.

### Can Buddy track expenses automatically?

Via bank import where supported. Lumi takes the no-bank route to automation: Apple Pay Shortcut import, voice parsing, and receipt scanning — no credentials shared with anyone.

### Which is better for couples?

Depends on the money model. One shared pot, planned together: Buddy. Separate accounts with shared costs settled monthly: Lumi — see [best budget apps for couples](/blog/best-budget-apps-for-couples-2026) for the full breakdown by model.

### Do both work offline?

Lumi is offline-first — data lives on the device. Buddy's shared features depend on cloud sync, so its collaborative core assumes connectivity.
