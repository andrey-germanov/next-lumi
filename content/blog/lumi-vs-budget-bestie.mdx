**Budget Bestie is the envelope method gone digital: allocate money into virtual envelopes each payday and spend only what's inside. Lumi is the opposite philosophy: capture real spending with near-zero effort, then let budgets and forecasts work on complete data.** Both fight overspending — one with pre-commitment, one with awareness. Price gap: ~$75/yr vs $39.99/yr.

## Quick comparison

| | Lumi | Budget Bestie |
|---|------|---------------|

| Entry methods | Voice, Apple Pay auto-import, receipt scan, manual | Manual entry into envelopes |
| Pay schedule support | Any — tracking is continuous | Weekly, biweekly, bimonthly, monthly cycles |

## Where Budget Bestie wins

For people who came from physical cash stuffing — the TikTok method with real envelopes and real bills — Budget Bestie is the honest digital translation: same pre-commitment psychology, no cash to carry, and flexible payday cycles (weekly, biweekly) that most budget apps ignore. Pre-allocating money *before* the month starts is a genuinely effective control for chronic overspending; it's the same principle as [the envelope method](/blog/what-is-the-envelope-method) and it works when followed.

## Where Lumi wins

Envelope systems have a known failure mode: every purchase needs manual filing into the right envelope, and the system only stays true while you keep filing. Lumi removes that step — voice logging, [automatic Apple Pay import](/blog/how-to-track-apple-pay-spending), and receipt scanning keep the record complete without the ritual. Category budgets give you the envelope-style limit (with warnings *before* you cross it), the [AI forecast](/blog/how-to-stop-overspending) shows where the month is heading, and the analytics actually answer "where did it go?" It's also nearly half the price, with a real free tier instead of a one-week trial.

## Which one should you pick?

Committed cash-stuffer who wants the psychology without the physical cash: Budget Bestie does that specific job well. Someone whose real problem is not knowing where money goes — or who has quit manual systems before: Lumi. And if it's the envelope *method* you want rather than the app, category budget limits reproduce it: set the limit, watch the live bar — [what is a spending limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit) explains the mechanics.

## FAQ

### How much does Budget Bestie cost?

Around $75/year (monthly and annual options, one-week free trial). That's roughly double Lumi Premium ($39.99/yr), and reviewers' most common hesitation is exactly that price-to-features ratio.

### Is cash stuffing better than expense tracking?

They target different failure modes. Cash stuffing prevents overspending by pre-commitment; tracking prevents it by awareness and early warning. People who abandon envelope filing tend to keep frictionless tracking — and vice versa.

### Does either app connect to a bank?

No. Budget Bestie skips bank sync; Lumi is no-bank by design and gets automation from voice, Apple Pay Shortcuts, and receipt scanning instead — data stays on your device.

### Can Lumi do envelope budgeting?

Functionally yes: category budget limits with live progress bars behave like envelopes that refill monthly — minus the manual allocation ritual. Free plan includes 2 budgets; Premium is unlimited.
