**A single person in Tbilisi spends about $1,000–1,500 per month including rent in 2026 — with a city-center one-bedroom at roughly $500–650 and everything else remaining some of the cheapest in any European capital-adjacent city.** Georgia's inflation has cooled to ~2.7%, so prices have stabilized. Here's the full picture.

## Rent takes half — everything else is small

Tbilisi's budget structure is unusual: rent eats 40–50% of the total, while almost every other line is tiny by Western standards — a $14 transport pass, $80 utilities, khachapuri and market produce keeping food cheap. That means the apartment decision *is* the budget decision: Vera and Vake carry the expat premium; Saburtalo delivers the same city for $100–200 less; and rent outside the center at ~$400 makes a $1,000 total genuinely comfortable.

## Food: market economics

Groceries at $150–250 assume the Dezerter Bazaar and local chains (Nikora, Ori Nabiji) over imported-goods supermarkets, where the same basket doubles. Eating out stays honest — a full khinkali dinner runs $10–15 — so the food budget's fate depends mostly on how often delivery apps and expat-priced brunch spots appear. That's [discretionary spending](/blog/what-is-discretionary-spending) doing its usual quiet work; a [category limit](/blog/what-is-a-spending-limit) on eating out keeps it visible.

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Tbilisi?

About $1,000–1,500/month for one person including rent in 2026. Under $1,000 is realistic outside the center with market-first food habits.

### Is Tbilisi cheap in 2026?

For daily life, yes — one of the best value propositions near Europe: $14 transport pass, $80 utilities, cheap excellent food. Rent is the exception, pushed up by remote-worker demand, though it remains far below EU capitals.

### How much is rent in Tbilisi?

Around $500–650 for a city-center one-bedroom (Vera, Vake, Old Town), ~$400 outside the center. Rent is typically 40–50% of a Tbilisi budget — an unusually high share because everything else is cheap.

### Do I need to budget in lari or dollars?

Spend in lari, think in your income currency — but track in both. Converting each purchase at the moment it happens (rather than reconstructing from statements) is the only way a multi-currency month produces one true total.
