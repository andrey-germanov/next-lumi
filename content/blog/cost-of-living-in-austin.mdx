**A realistic all-in budget for a single person in Austin runs $2,300–3,300 per month in 2026 — with a median one-bedroom around $1,245 and *falling*, thanks to a construction boom that pushed vacancy to 16.7%.** Austin flipped from America's hottest market to a genuine renter's market. Here's the breakdown and the catches.

## Austin monthly budget at a glance

| Category | Monthly cost (USD) |
|----------|--------------------|
| 1-bedroom, downtown/South Congress | $1,400–1,800 |
| 1-bedroom, North Austin/Riverside/suburbs | $950–1,300 |
| Car: payment, insurance, gas | $550–750 |
| Groceries (single person) | $400–550 |
| Utilities + internet (summer AC heavy) | $180–280 |
| Eating out (moderate) | $250–450 |
| Daily costs ex-rent (incl. car) | ~$1,500 |
| **Realistic total** | **$2,300–3,300** |

Sources: [ApartmentAdvisor](https://www.apartmentadvisor.com/national-rent-report), [Apartments.com rent report](https://www.apartments.com/blog/apartments.com-national-rent-trends-report-january-2026), [Numbeo](https://www.numbeo.com/cost-of-living/in/Austin) — mid-2026 figures.

## Rent: the renter's market is real — negotiate

Austin's 2024–25 apartment construction boom outran demand: 16.7% vacancy means concessions are back — free months, waived fees, negotiable renewals. The median $1,245 one-bedroom is a boom-town artifact you should actively exploit: ask for two months free on a 14-month lease, and re-shop at every renewal. Rents this soft are a savings-rate opportunity ([the by-30 math](/blog/how-much-savings-by-30) loves a $300 rent cut).

## The Texas trade: no income tax, hot everything

No state income tax nets a $90k earner ~$350–500/month over California pricing. The offsets: summer electricity ($250–350 in July–August is normal — budget the peak, bank the rest), property taxes that landlords pass through, and a car for nearly everyone ($550–750/month). The car is the quiet half of "cheap Austin" — factor it before celebrating the rent.

## Cost of living in Austin for a single person vs a family of 4

Single: $2,300–3,300, with the downtown-vs-suburbs choice worth ~$500/month. Family of four: 3BR at $1,800–2,600, two cars, $1,000–1,300 groceries, childcare $1,000–1,500 per child — totals $6,000–8,500. That's [national-average territory](/blog/average-monthly-expenses-2026) with a tech-city amenity set; the [50/30/20 split](/tools/50-30-20-budget-calculator) fits without straining.

## FAQ

### How much money do you need to live in Austin?

$2,300–3,300/month all-in for a single person in 2026; sub-$2,000 is possible with roommates in a soft rental market.

### Is Austin still expensive?

It's cheaper than it was — rents fell from the 2022 peak as vacancy hit 16.7%. Against coastal tech cities it's a bargain; against 2019 Austin it isn't.

### What salary do you need for Austin?

$60–80k covers a comfortable solo setup with a car; no state income tax stretches it further than the sticker suggests.

### What surprises people about Austin costs?

Summer electric bills, property-tax pass-through in rent, car dependence — and how negotiable everything became once vacancy spiked.
